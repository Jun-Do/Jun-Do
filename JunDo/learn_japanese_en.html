Here you can learn the basics of Japanese in order to communicate in Japan.

Anki allows you to learn vocabulary in the form of flashcards. The less you master a word, the more it will come back.

Vocabulary

Konnichiwa ! (こんにちは)
Hello !
Ohayô gozaimasu (おはようございます)
Good morning
(le matin)
Konbanha (今晩は)
Good afternoon
Sayônara (左様なら)
Goodbye
Onegai shimasu(お願いします)
please
Arigatô (有難う) / Dômo (どうも)
Thanks (neutral)
Arigatô gozaimasu (有難うございます) / Dômo arigatô (どうも有難う)
Thanks (polite)
Dômo arigatô gozaimasu (どうもありがとうございます)
Thanks (very polite).
Hai (はい)
Yes
Iee (いええ)
No
Watashi wa Pierre desu. (私はピエルです)
My name is Pierre.

Tips

  • the "r" in Japanese is always pronounced "l".
    example: the word "ramen" that we are used to pronounce with an "r" is actually pronounced "lamen".
  • The letters "ô" and "û" in Japanese are extended and counted as two syllables. The difference is more obvious in music, where the syllables are well separated.
  • the "su" at the end of verbs is pronounced "ss" (verbs are words ending in "-masu" and the word "desu").
